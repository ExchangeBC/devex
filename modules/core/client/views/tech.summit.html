In an effort to make it faster and easier for government and developers to work together, the BCDevExchange has created two new procurement processes.

Code With Us drives government to develop its digital products in the open and enables government to instantly reach a large community of talented software developers and quickly acquire enhancements to government digital products.

So far, over $700,000 worth of small opportunities ranging from $1,000 - $70,000 have been awarded to developers, many of whom have never worked for government.

Sprint With Us enables government to hire an entire software development team within 30 calendar days for contracts of up to 2 years or $2M. Teams who apply get the opportunity to strut their stuff through a code challenge and in-person scenario. And under a new Agile Software Development Agreement, teams are freed to bring their innovation to shape the product, rather than being locked into delivering on a pre-determined set of requirements.

Come learn about these new opportunities and how you can apply. If you’ve already applied, we'll be keen to hear about your experience and learn how we can improve.

The Design System is a collection of digital resources and tools - including reusable UI components and design patterns -- aimed to help developers and designers build custom B.C government products.

The goal of the design system is to makes it easier and faster to build B.C. government products while creating a consistent experience across services.

Components are collectively contributed to from the government community. They are built to meet accessibility standards and are open for input and improvement. Building in the open ensures this resource is always relevant and up to date with the latest needs from the community.

Virtual Reality – Weather Real-time data demo

The Integrated Data Division has partnered with LlamaZOO, through a Code With Us opportunity, to develop a prototype real-time data API service lightning strike and wildfire simulated data and Virtual Reality demonstration of the use of the APIs.

The project aims to help us understand the value of open data innovation in solving public sector challenges, increase awareness of new approaches to data visualization in the public sector and create new approaches to deliver real-time data through APIs for data visualization platforms.
